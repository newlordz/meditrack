Scientific Analysis: RX-99120-B

Mechanism of Interaction

Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) like Ibuprofen may enhance the anticoagulant effect of Warfarin. This occurs through several mechanisms: displacement of Warfarin from protein binding sites, interference with platelet function, and potential induction of gastric mucosal injury which increases bleeding risk.

Clinical Significance

Studies indicate a 3.3-fold increase in the risk of GI bleeding when these agents are co-administered. The effect on INR may also be unpredictable.

Evidence Level

Level A: Extensive clinical research and documented case studies.
